How To Protect Your Personal Data?

June 14, 2018 | By: Nicholas Wyrick

Social media sites such as Facebook Shares a lot of personal data including your family, your friends,and information about yourself. It's best not to post anything personal or your location and even your family photos. Facebook is a data mine for hackers across the world. It's the best place to gather information about someone you don't even know.

Some people have posted their new drivers ID card within a picture without realizing how much data they just gave out. Some Have posted pictures of them buying a new house with the address linked in the description.

When it comes to social media sites it's best to leave your personal life offline. People such as teens and adults that are inexperienced don't even see how much personal data they are giving out while posting online.

"I don’t think that I’ll ever feel safe online."

Q. How safe do you feel personally online?

A. I would never say that I am un-hackable. Data breaches are occurring at such a constant rate, it feels like a norm, and for that reason, I don’t think that I’ll ever feel safe online. Therefore, I take precautions to protect myself as much as possible.

Q. What should people never do on social media?

A. Post without thinking. Period. Before you post something, ask yourself these questions: What information am I putting online? What is in the background of my image? If I wanted revenge on myself ― how would I use this information against me?

Q. In your opinion, which social media site exposes our vulnerability the most?

A. I think Facebook exposes the most information ― mainly because Facebook correlates a huge amount of data, such as your friends, co-workers, family, your job, your hobbies, your kids, etc. Many answers to security questions [used for bank transactions and password resets] can be found just by looking at someone’s Facebook account.

  • When using a public computer or a friend’s personal computer always check for key loggers.
